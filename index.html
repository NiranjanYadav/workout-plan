FRIDAY — REST or Badminton

Active recovery • Light stretching, foam rolling, or badminton session

SATURDAY — Badminton

Cardio & Agility • 60-90 min session

  • Badminton session: 60-90 minutes of doubles or singles play
  • Great for: cardio, agility, footwork, HIIT-like intensity
  • Calorie burn: 400-600 cal/session
  • Also counts toward 10K daily steps

SUNDAY — Badminton or REST

Play or recover based on how you feel

  • Option A: Badminton session (60-90 min) if energy and recovery allow
  • Option B: Full rest day — light walking, stretching, foam rolling
  • Listen to your body: If legs are sore from Thursday Pull B, rest and recover
  • Either way: Hit your 10K steps and 140g protein

General Guidelines

  • Warm up 5-10 minutes before each session (light cardio + dynamic stretches).
  • Progressive overload: Increase weight by 2-5% when you complete all sets at the top of the rep range.
  • Rest between sets: Compound lifts (squat, deadlift, bench) = 90s-3min. Isolation = 45-60s.
  • Tempo: 2 seconds down, 1 second pause, 1 second up (controlled movements).
  • Breathing: Exhale on exertion (the hard part), inhale on the eccentric (lowering).
  • Hydration: Drink 500ml-1L water per session.
  • Friday is a rest or light badminton day. Stretching and foam rolling recommended.
  • Track your weights in the log tables to monitor progress week over week.
  • Core work is built into Tuesday and Wednesday — no separate core day needed.
  • Badminton on weekends provides cardio — no treadmill needed.
  • 10K steps daily — non-negotiable, rain or shine.
  • Target: 140g protein daily, 1,800-2,000 calories for cut.

FRIDAY — Badminton (1 hour)

Legs 48hr recovered from Wednesday — ready to play

  • Duration: 60 minutes — doubles or singles
  • Why this slot: Wednesday's leg day has full 48hr recovery before badminton
  • Calorie burn: 400-500 cal/session
  • Hydration: 1L water during session, electrolytes if sweating heavily
  • Watch for: Knee/ankle fatigue from squats — warm up extra carefully

SATURDAY — Badminton (2 hours)

Long session — legs fully recovered (72hr from Wed)

  • Duration: 120 minutes — longer doubles session, multiple games
  • Calorie burn: 800-1000 cal/session
  • Hydration: 2-3L water, definitely electrolytes
  • Eat carbs before: Banana + coffee 30 min pre-session for energy
  • If body feels destroyed: Cap at 1hr. Sunday rest is non-negotiable.

SUNDAY — Full REST

The only true rest day — protect it

  • No gym, no badminton, no intense activity
  • OK: Light walk for 10K steps, stretching, foam rolling, sauna
  • Protein: Still hit 170g target — recovery happens on rest days
  • Sleep: Aim for 7.5+ hours — this is when muscle repair happens
  • Check HRV Monday morning: If <38 ms, take Monday as additional rest day

v2 Plan — Critical Reminders

  • Protein: 170g/day MINIMUM. Whey 25g post-workout + 3 protein meals. Current 125g avg is causing 1:1 lean:fat loss.
  • Calories: 1,800-2,000 (lift days), 1,500-1,700 (rest days). Don't drop below 1,500.
  • Sodium/Potassium: 2,000-2,500 mg Na, 3,500+ mg K. Restaurant meals cap = 1/week.
  • HRV check: Apr 25 baseline = 41 ms. Target 48+ ms by May 22 (Mg supplement working). If drops below 38, extra rest day.
  • Weekly weigh-in: Friday morning, post-bathroom, pre-food. Compare 7-day rolling averages.
  • Progressive overload: Add 2.5kg when hitting top of rep range with good form. Track every session.
  • Deload week: Every 4-6 weeks, drop to 50% volume for 1 week.
  • Form > Weight: Always. Especially RDL, squat, bench. Watch videos before each session if unsure.
